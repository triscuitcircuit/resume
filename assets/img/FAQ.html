1. Facts and Questions

1.1. Why Maine for technology?

Maine colleges have had a record enrollment in present years, leading to a massive increase in students studying IT and computer science. According to the U.S. Bureau of labor statistics, projected percent change in employment growth for Computer and Information research is 22% – compared to the average of 8% growth across all sectors. Due to this, it's expected that more technical degrees will be given out by Maine colleges in the coming years. This will lead to an increase in talented individuals looking for jobs in the area.

1.2. Tech space competition

As it currently stands, there aren't many technology focused companies currently in Maine. If a major company where to move into the area, a lot of fresh talent would be available employment. As it stands, Software development jobs are the fastest growing jobs in Maine (Zippia) at a growth of a predicted rate of 116%.

1.3. How is an increase of technology jobs going to impact local residents?

Though they add some strain to local communities, places in Maine will benefit from the increased innovation (nytimes). With more college graduates, new technology based businesses will be tempted to move to Maine. With the increase of workers staying in Maine for technology based jobs, it will boost the local economy with an increased labor pool and by increasing customers.

1.4. Why Tech jobs specifically?

From 2010-2019, technology based jobs grew by 25% in Maine, and grew by 4.1% in 2019 from the previous year (comptia). With the use of technology and recent innovations during the pandemic, the demand for jobs in this field has only increased. Computer technology is also constantly improving with new hardware being introduced in the market that can accomplish more compared to previous generations.
